Why Do I Need to Learn How To Code?
By Steven Dewey, March 15, 2014

Why is learning to code important?

Learning to code is about more than building software or websites. It is literally about beginning to understand the world around you. Almost everything we do during our day involves some sort of code. Whether that is answering the phone, checking email, watching T.V., even driving your car! The future will belong to those individuals who understand how the world works. A super detailed and specialized understanding of coding is not completely necessary, however, the more specialized skills you posses the greater impact you can make in the world around you. Knowing how to code will at a bare minimum allow you to engage intellectually with those who do understand it in depth. It will allow you to understand constraints and possibilities that face current developers. It could be that small differentiator that sets you apart from the rest of your peers.

The world of technology is like a thriving city with giant walls around the outside. Only qualified individuals are able to pass through the gates to unlimited opportunity and prosperity. The qualification is knowing how to code.

Don’t get left out side the gates of the city of technology. Learn to code today!

“Our policy at Facebook is literally to hire as many talented engineers as we can find. There just aren't enough people who are trained and have these skills today.”

Mark Zuckerberg | Founder, Facebook

You may say that coding does not apply to my career, profession or industry. Although that may be true right now, I am sure you have already noticed automated systems coming into your workplace, software, applications, programs that make your job easier for you to do. The more complex and more integrated these systems get the more valuable will be the technical understanding of how these systems work. Save your self the struggle of mis-understanding computer systems and software. Start learning how it works, increase your knowledge, you will never regret it. Even some NBA basketball players are learning how to code because of it's relevance to EVERY career, why wouldn’t you?
